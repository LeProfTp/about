001 — Work

Exploring how decentralised systems — from Bitcoin to startup ecosystems to human networks — support adaptation in a complex world.

Over more than 20 years, Thomas Petzold has worked at the intersection of social and technological innovation — with research and industry partners across Europe, the Asia-Pacific, and beyond. His work connects network science, trust research, and media management to produce findings that reach practitioners as well as peers.

Alumnus of the Oxford Internet Institute and Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. PhD, Queensland University of Technology. Reviewer for SAGE, Elsevier. International board member, Cultural Science Journal.

002 — Research

Four
pillars

01

Network Science & Economics

How complex networks shape innovation, information diffusion, and economic outcomes. Applied to media markets, knowledge flows, and management strategy.

02

Trust Research — Blockchain & AI

Trust-verification principles in blockchain ecosystems and AI assistants. How reliable knowledge and action can be established in machine-mediated environments.

03

Solution-Oriented Management

Research that goes beyond citation counts — a framework for impact-focused work that connects academic findings to real changes in practice and policy.

04

Entrepreneurship & Startup Ecosystems

How structural positioning in networks shapes entrepreneurial outcomes. From ecosystem design to startup mentoring — bridging research and the practice of building.

Trust Machines Lab — dedicated research unit for blockchain ecosystems, generative AI, DAOs and distributed trust
